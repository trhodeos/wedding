About Us

The short version of the story is we were best friends who decided to get married. The long version is a little bit more interesting. It all started when Melissa, Shradha and I needed a new roommate. Shradha and Tyler knew each other from days together in UT Natural Science Council, and he was brave enough to say he was willing to live with 3 girls. Tyler moved in during the summer when Shradha and Melissa were living in Houston, so we got to know each other really well. We bonded over our love for tex mex, good tv shows, and the search for a dog for Tyler to adopt (which we were super successful with because Scout is the best dog ever). I joked that he was basically my boyfriend because we did everything together. Little did I know 6 months later we would be dating, and 2 years later we would be engaged and living in San Francisco!

How He Proposed

Tyler proposed on February 22, 2014 in Austin and it was a complete surprise. I was under the impression that we were going to a boat party on Town Lake to celebrate our friend, Sid. How this wasn’t suspicious to me, I have no idea! We spent the day like any other day: doing chores around the house and grabbing a late lunch at South Congress Cafe. As we were heading to the dock, Tyler started to stall and said we needed to grab coffee because he was tired. Apparently, our friends weren’t done decorating the boat for our arrival and told him to wait. I was super worried that we would miss the boat, but still wasn’t catching on to the ploy. Once we make our way down to the dock, Tyler tells me that we have to take a practice run on the boat before the party gets there. The boat was decorated with white streamers and had two glasses of champagne...still I don’t suspect a thing other than they had very questionable decorations for a boat party for a guy! We make our way out to the middle of the lake where it all clicks as he stops the boat and pulls the ring out. Of course I said YES! We went back around and picked up some of our closest friends and called our family. The surprises didn’t stop there because little did I know that both of our families were back at the house to surprise us with an engagement party. It was the absolute best.
